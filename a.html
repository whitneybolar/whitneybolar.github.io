Information

In our research, we’ve found that there are several common problems that users face with this HP Envy x360, and addressed the following topics on our troubleshooting page:

● Screen cracked or broken

● Short battery life

● Heating problems resulting in a loud fan

● Random restarts or burning smell

● Programs open very slowly


We created replacement guides for the following components:

● Screen

● Battery

● Fan

● Motherboard

● Hard Drive

Usability Testing

Usability Set Up:

Each participant was provided the aforementioned laptop for repairs, computer access to the replacement guides, and an iFixit toolkit. One team member verbally outlined the test instructions while another took notes of performance during execution of the guides as well as timed the participants.


Usability Feedback:

Upon completion, both participants commented that the replacement guides were straightforward and easy to comprehend. However, there were a few notes that were worth reporting:

● For hard drive, we did not indicate to “pry up latch” connecting the white ribbon cable to the motherboard

● For the motherboard, one participant had difficulty locating the power jack based off the photo used in guide

● For the screen, the first step to “remove 2 screws” from the guide was omitted

● For the screen, we will consider marking up the “black tape” as a contingency

● For the screen, we did not mention to disconnect the golden connector after removing the UPC tape

● Generally speaking, participant #2 did not fully read instructions before advancing and needed to backtrack multiple times before moving forward

Results

Purpose of the project:

In order to help create repair guides for electronics to aid in the reduction of global e-waste.


Our client:

This project provides a symbiotic relationship for iFixit, students, and professors. iFixit is able to generate repair guides for their business at the cost of providing electronic devices, toolkits, and tech support, while students and professors are able to use real world applications to aid in learning technical writing.


What we learned about teamwork:

My recommendation for the project is that groups be divided up randomly or some other measure than by academic prowess. It was blatantly evident that certain groups were short on manpower and ultimately I believe they could have used some strongly performing students to keep them accountable.
